Nowadays, it’s pretty simple to pretend to be a person or corporation on the web. By copying your name and logo, a criminal can create fake social profiles with ease, damaging your online reputation and bruising your brand’s carefully crafted image.

In order to seem trustworthy, criminals act like they’re a part of a reliable, recognized institution. Using emails, text messages, or fake social profiles, they invite users to access a fake site—a site that’s nearly identical to the original.

Coming from an unexpected external attack or even an accidentally negligent employee, sensitive and classified information leakage in places like cyberlockers or file repositories can mean serious damage to your credibility.

The security of your executives’ sensitive data should be a top priority. After all, the unintentional (or intentional) disclosure of private information like names, addresses, phone numbers, and email addresses can be catastrophic. Ultimately, it makes it easier for criminals to impersonate VIP executives in social media and across the web.

In the current world of digital commerce, abuse in online sales can take many forms. Whether it looks like selling a counterfeit purse or illegal medications, unauthorized sales of your genuine product in an unregulated channel, or a seller impersonating your entire brand, your business will take a massive credibility hit from these imposters.

ON THE WEB IT’S NOT ALWAYS EASY TO KNOW WHERE YOU’RE GOING…

The web is the very first place your clients head to search for you, and it’s also where they’ll encounter the most direct threats against the brand you’ve worked so hard to build.

For example, since it’s not always easy for a customer to know if a website is official (or not), constant monitoring can prevent your potential customers from being mislead by criminals.

THE FAMILIARITY OF SOCIAL MEDIA PROVIDES A FALSE SENSE OF SECURITY.

While social media accounts are one of the most relevant aspects of the overall digital presence for your brand, they’re also incredibly vulnerable.

Without proper monitoring, they become susceptible to account takeovers, impersonations, hashtag hijacking, and product scams—all of which can deeply damage the image of your brand and break the trust between your company and the public.

MOBILE IS NOW GROUND ZERO FOR SCAM & BRAND ATTACKS.

Scammers and cybercriminals use a wide variety of tactics to scam the community of users. By impersonating other people, embedding malware into mobile devices via SMS texts (known as smishing), or offering rogue apps, the reach of their negative influence is widespread.

Phishing scams targeting mobile devices are on the rise since it can be hard to recognize a fake website on such a tiny screen, and the web pages are usually different from the desktop version—both of which make fraud easier to commit.

COMPLETELY CONTROL THE ONLINE BUYING EXPERIENCE OF YOUR PRODUCTS.

Without the proper tools, it’s virtually impossible to control how your products are sold in marketplaces, webshops or social media.

Unfortunately, criminals and opportunist sellers are aware of that fact too, so selling counterfeit or official products in an unregulated channel is one of the easiest ways to make money online.

EMAIL’S THE EASIEST WAY TO CONNECT TO CUSTOMERS—FOR YOU AND THE CRIMINALS.

Protecting that hard-earned trust between your company and the people you reach via e-mail is tantamount. As companies strive to leverage their digital presence to connect with more customers and gain more control in their target market, shielding the company's email reputation takes on a greater importance.

After all, when a fake, scammy or unauthorized email reaches your customers, the results can be absolutely disastrous.

FINALLY SHINE A LIGHT ON THE DARK WEB.

A gigantic mass of data and people that communicate and trade in the underbelly of the mainstream web most of us know. The actions taking place there are “invisible” and “untraceable” for most people. In this environment there are no restrictions, and it’s an uncharted territory where people indulge in illicit acts without being discovered.

Criminals use these spaces to perform and sell all sorts of crimes, ranging from the trade of stolen credit cards to sensitive data about executives.
